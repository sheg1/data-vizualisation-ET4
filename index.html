DATA VIZUALISATION FINAL PROJECT

The Price of Life: Does More Spending Buy More Time?

An interactive investigation into the diminishing returns of global healthcare expenditure and systemic efficiency.

01. The Healthcare Plateau

Does a higher budget always translate to a longer life? This visualization aggregates global data into spending tiers to reveal the overall trend. We observe a sharp initial increase followed by a clear "saturation point".

Visual Encoding: We used an Area + Line mark to represent the "mass" of the data. The Y-axis represents the mean life expectancy per tier. The solid shape emphasizes the plateau effect more effectively than a simple scatter plot.

02. Measuring Efficiency (Marginal Gains)

To answer our research question about diminishing returns, we calculated the Marginal Gain: how many additional years of life are gained for every extra $1,000 spent.

Visual Encoding: Bar Marks were used to compare relative differences between tiers. Blue indicates high efficiency (>1 year gained), while Red signals the zone of diminishing returns.

Critical Thought & Limitations

Findings: Our analysis reveals that health spending is vital for low-income nations (gaining +20 years for the first $1k), but becomes extremely inefficient past the $5,000 mark.

Limitations:

  • Outliers: Some tiers (like $10k+) are dominated by a single country (USA), skewing the mean.
  • Purchasing Power: The data doesn't account for the local cost of medical services.
  • Data Lag: Reporting delays in some regions may affect the most recent tiers.
